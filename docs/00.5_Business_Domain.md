Purpose

This document defines the business language used throughout Dhanashree ERP.

Every database table, API endpoint, frontend component, AI workflow, report, and business rule must use the terminology defined here.

This document serves as the single source of truth for understanding how the contractor business operates.

⸻

Domain Overview

The business consists of five primary operational domains.

Client
↓
Project
↓
Execution
↓
Finance
↓
Completion

Every action performed inside the software belongs to one of these domains.

Core Business Entities

⸻

Company

The contractor organization operating the software.

Example:

* Dhanashree Trading Company

Responsibilities

* Owns projects
* Pays suppliers
* Pays labour
* Receives payments from clients

A company owns every other entity inside the system.

⸻

Client

A customer who awards construction or fabrication work.

Examples

* Vasavi Homes
* Aparna Constructions
* My Home Group

A client may own multiple projects.

A project belongs to exactly one client.

⸻

Project

A project is the central entity of the application.

Every operational activity should belong to a project.

A project contains:

* Work Order
* Quotations
* Transactions
* Labour
* Suppliers
* Documents
* Progress
* Reports
* Analytics

Without a project, operational activities cannot exist.

⸻

Work Order

A work order is the official authorization provided by a client to begin execution.

A work order contains:

* Scope of work
* Area
* Agreed rate
* Payment terms
* Project location
* Client information

A work order creates a project.

⸻

Quotation

A quotation is a commercial proposal submitted before project approval.

Status

* Draft
* Submitted
* Revised
* Accepted
* Rejected

Once accepted, it becomes the financial reference for the project.

⸻

Transaction

A transaction represents every movement of money.

This is the most important entity in the system.

Examples

Income

* Advance received
* Final payment
* Refund

Expense

* Material purchase
* Labour salary
* Miscellaneous
* Fuel
* Transport
* Equipment
* Accommodation

Every transaction belongs to exactly one project unless explicitly marked as a company-wide expense.

Transactions are immutable financial records.

⸻

Payment

A payment is the settlement of a transaction.

Example

Transaction

Purchase Steel

₹68,000

Payment

Paid ₹40,000

Remaining

₹28,000

One transaction may have multiple payments.

⸻

Supplier

A supplier provides goods or services.

Examples

* Steel supplier
* Paint supplier
* Hardware shop
* Transport vendor

Supplier Ledger

Purchased

↓

Paid

↓

Outstanding Balance

Every supplier maintains a running ledger.

⸻

Material

Physical items consumed during execution.

Examples

* MS Tubes
* Corrugated Sheets
* Putty
* Screws
* Paint
* Cement

Materials are linked to purchase transactions.

⸻

Labour

A worker performing project activities.

Labour information includes:

* Name
* Contact
* Daily Wage
* Attendance
* Night Shift
* Sunday Shift
* Advances
* Salary History

Labour is assigned to one or more projects.

⸻

Attendance

Attendance records labour participation.

Attendance is never financial.

Attendance generates salary calculations.

Attendance types

* Full Day
* Half Day
* Night Shift
* Sunday Shift
* Leave
* Absent

⸻

Salary

Salary is generated from attendance.

Salary is never entered manually.

Salary Formula

Working Days

Night Bonus

Sunday Bonus

Advance Paid

=

Salary Due

⸻

Miscellaneous Expense

A miscellaneous expense represents operational spending outside standard material procurement.

Examples

* Tea
* Water
* Petrol
* Parking
* Screws
* Blades
* Tape
* Auto Fare
* Food
* Emergency Purchase

Miscellaneous expenses are one of the primary causes of hidden project costs.

Therefore they are treated as first-class financial entities.

⸻

Document

Documents provide evidence for business activities.

Types

* Work Order
* Quotation
* Invoice
* GST Bill
* Receipt
* Delivery Challan
* Site Photo
* Completion Certificate

Every financial transaction should be traceable to at least one document whenever possible.

⸻

Cashflow

Cashflow represents the movement of money over time.

Cashflow consists of

Cash In

* Client Payments
* Refunds

Cash Out

* Materials
* Labour
* Transport
* Miscellaneous
* Equipment

Cashflow is always calculated.

It is never stored.

⸻

Profit

Profit represents the financial outcome of a project.

Formula

Revenue

Expenses

=

Profit

Profit is always calculated dynamically.

⸻

Domain Rules

Rule 1

Everything should belong to a Project whenever possible.

⸻

Rule 2

No financial record may exist without a Transaction.

⸻

Rule 3

Transactions cannot be deleted.

Only reversed or corrected.

⸻

Rule 4

Attendance never stores salary.

Attendance generates salary.

⸻

Rule 5

Profit is never manually entered.

⸻

Rule 6

Cashflow is never stored.

Always calculated.

⸻

Rule 7

Documents never change financial values directly.

They create or verify transactions.

⸻

Rule 8

Payments settle transactions.

They do not replace them.

The following terminology must be used consistently throughout the project.

Business Term	Meaning

Client	Customer awarding the project
Work Order	Official authorization to start work
Quotation	Commercial proposal before approval
Transaction	Financial movement
Payment	Settlement of a transaction
Supplier	Vendor providing goods or services
Labour	Worker executing work
Attendance	Daily labour record
Salary	Payment calculated from attendance
Material	Physical item consumed during work
Misc Expense	Operational expense outside standard procurement
Cashflow	Movement of money over time

Conclusion

This Business Domain Model defines the common language of Dhanashree ERP.

All future architecture, database schemas, APIs, frontend components, reports, AI modules, and documentation must align with the concepts and rules established in this document.

Maintaining consistency with this ubiquitous language ensures the platform remains scalable, maintainable, and understandable as it evolves from a single-company solution into a full contractor operating system.
